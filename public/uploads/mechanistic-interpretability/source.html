MechInterp in Transformers – What is a feature?: Linear directions in activation space encode interpretable meanings (Mikolov et al., 2013); The linear representation hypothesis holds in LLMs (Park et al., 2024); Linear probes can read off internal representations, e.g. world models in Othello-GPT (Nanda et al., 2023); Steering vectors (Panickssery et al., 2024); Ablation of single directions can remove capabilities like refusal (Arditi et al., 2024). Teaching guide - Present the slides, with the lecture notes in the pdf using pdfpc to make the speaker notes visible to yourself but not the participants. - Separate the group into tiers based on previous exposure to mechinterp concepts. When asking a question: in general let the tiers with less exposure answer first. - Whenever you hit a slide with links to exercises, let the participants do the exercises. For the links to neuropedia, give them 10-20 min to look around on neuropedia, find interesting features/circuits and get a hands on feeling for how these look like. Key takeaways: many features are mislabeled by the LLMs or still seem polysemantic; features generated with circuit tracing look extremely messy and are not straight forward to interpret. - After Lecture and Exercises, people should separate into groups, each reading and then discussing one of the MechInterp Criticism posts linked above. If enough time is left, one person from each discussion group can present a summary of the post and their discussion to the rest of class.
