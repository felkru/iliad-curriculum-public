B.3 Singular Learning Theory Contributors Teaching plan and lecture notes by: - Kai Ogden (University of Oxford) - Matthew Farrugia-Roberts (University of Oxford) - Zach Furman (The University of Melbourne) Lecture slides by Zach Furman. Learning outcomes After completing this module, the students should be able to: 1. Define degeneracy of parameter–function maps and identify degenerate directions in simple models (including deep linear networks and multi-layer perceptrons). 2. Define the local learning coefficient via volume scaling asymptotics, compute learning coefficients in simple examples, and compare to the regular and minimally singular cases. 3. State Watanabe's free energy formula and analyse toy examples of Bayesian phase transitions. Prerequisites The following is an indicative list of mathematical concepts that will be helpful for reading the tutorial and completing the exercises. - Linear algebra: vectors, matrices, rank, orthogonal matrices, rank–nullity, positive definiteness, eigenvalues, spectral decomposition. - Calculus: partial derivatives, gradient, directional derivative, chain rule, Hessian, second-order Taylor expansion and remainder. - Integration and analysis: multivariate integrals, change of variables, volume in R^d, asymptotic notation (big-O, little-o), computing basic limits and integrals. - Probability: probability simplex, conditional probability, probability density functions, independence, expectation, Bayes' rule, Gaussians, law of large numbers. Resources for most of these prerequisites are listed in either the ARENA Prerequisites: Core concepts or the prerequisites tab. We also require some specific terminology and notation regarding deep learning, optimisation, and statistical inference: - Deep learning: parametric function approximation or statistical inference (parameter–function maps, deep linear networks, multi-layer perceptrons, loss functions, likelihood). - Bayesian statistics: prior, posterior, partition function, Bayesian free energy. These topics are also mentioned in Module 0 or the ARENA materials, and some of the material will overlap with Modules B.1 and B.2 overviewing deep learning theory. The lecture notes for this module also review everything we need, and introduce our notation, in section 1 ("Preliminaries"). Readers with more advanced backgrounds may appreciate occasional references throughout the lecture notes on topics from algebraic geometry, fractal geometry, or statistical physics. Content Fast track To paraphrase Euclid, there is no royal road to algebro-geometric learning theory. However, it is possible to get a bird's-eye view and the most important intuitions in a comparably short time. Assuming you are already somewhat comfortable with deep learning and Bayesian inference, skip Section 1 of the lecture notes, and refer back only as needed. Then, proceed as follows: 1. To understand parameter–function map versus loss landscape degeneracy: Read Section 2.1 and complete Exercises 2.1 and 2.2. Complete either Exercise 2.9 or 2.10. Read Section 2.5 and complete your choice of Exercises 2.14 and/or 2.15. 2. To understand the local learning coefficient via volume scaling: Read Section 3.1 and complete Exercises 3.1, 3.2, 3.4, and 3.7. Read Section 3.3 and Exercise 3.10. 3. To understand the relation between degeneracy and learning in the Bayesian case: Read all of Section 4 (it is shorter). Complete Exercise 4.2. Teaching guide Day plan (ILO = Intended learning outcome; exercises with * = 'if time allows'; all exercise numbers from the Spring 2026 pilot notes). Block 1 (10:00–11:00) — Introduction to singular learning theory: The puzzle of implicit model selection; preliminaries; defining degeneracy; local learning coefficient definition, intuitions; Bayesian learning; free energy formula; Bayesian phase transitions; resolving the puzzle; empirical work. ILOs 1,2,3. Block 2 (11:00–12:30) — What is degeneracy? Notes: Sections 1.1, 1.2, 2. Exercises: (1) Definition of degeneracy (ex. 2.1, 2.2, 2.3*); (2) Examples from deep learning (ex. 2.6*, 2.9, 2.10*); (3) Relating different definitions of degeneracy (ex. 2.12*, 2.15). ILO 1. Exercises: (1) Volume scaling (ex. 3.1, 3.2); (2) Learning coefficient via volume scaling (ex. 3.4, 3.5*); (3) Learning coefficient examples (ex. 3.7, 3.10). ILO 2. Afternoon break (15:30–16:00). Format: Lecture blocks deliver slides with pauses for student questions. Exercise blocks: TAs give a brief (10 min) oral/whiteboard overview of the block's theme and point students at the relevant section of the notes; set first exercise(s), give ~20 minutes to work, TAs walk around and help; give a brief (5–10 min) oral/whiteboard recap, set next exercise(s), repeat. Aim for ~1 TA per 10 students, fully available.
