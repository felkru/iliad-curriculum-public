### A definition

<Definition term="Inductive bias">
  The set of assumptions a learner uses to generalise from finitely many
  examples to unseen ones. Two learners with identical training data can
  disagree because their inductive biases differ.
</Definition>

### A formal statement

<Theorem kind="theorem" name="Universal approximation">
  For any continuous $f : [0,1]^n \to \mathbb{R}$ and any $\epsilon > 0$, there
  exists a one-hidden-layer neural network $\hat f$ with $\sup_x |f(x) - \hat f(x)| < \epsilon$.
</Theorem>

Use `kind="lemma"`, `"proposition"`, or `"corollary"` for the obvious
variants. The optional `name` attribution shows in the header.

  non-isolated minimum.
</Exercise>

<Solution>
  Take $f_\{\theta\}(x) = \theta_1 \theta_2 x$ and $\ell(y, \hat y) = (y - \hat y)^2$. Then the locus $\{ \theta_1 \theta_2 = c \}$ is a continuum of
