## Overview

This module explores the training dynamics of neural networks through two interlinked themes: **implicit regularization** — the inductive biases that gradient-based optimization imposes on over-parameterized models — and **emergence** — the puzzle of capabilities that appear abruptly during training. Deep linear networks (DLNs) serve as the central toy model, alongside grokking, induction heads, and emergent misalignment, with dynamical mean field theory introduced as a framework for understanding training-time phase transitions.

## Prerequisites

-   Basics of deep learning (SGD, network, loss function)
    
-   Transformer architectures and LLMs
    
-   Statistics
    
-   Linear algebra (singular value decomposition)
    
-   Ordinary differential equations
    
-   May help but not strictly required: Group actions, Stochastic differential equations, Statistical physics and statistical field theory
