## Overview

Mechanistic interpretability (mechinterp) seeks to reverse-engineer the internal computations of neural networks into human-understandable features and circuits. This module surveys the goals of the field, the methods used for feature and circuit discovery, the current frontier of what we can and cannot interpret, and the major critiques of the field's theories of impact.

## Prerequisites

-   Knowing the basics of Machine Learning
    
-   Familiarity with the Transformer Architecture
    
-   Basic maths / linear algebra

**Selected readings**

Motivation and Foundations:

-   Goal of mechanistic interpretability and its relevance for AI safety
    
-   AI safety ontology: features and circuits as the building blocks of neural network understanding ([Olah et al., 2020](https://distill.pub/2020/circuits/zoom-in))
    

Historical MechInterp:

-   Feature visualization and saliency maps in CNNs
    
-   Circuits in CNNs: how features compose into higher-level detectors ([Cammarata et al., 2020](https://distill.pub/2020/circuits/curve-detectors))
    

MechInterp in Transformers – What is a feature?

-   Linear directions in activation space encode interpretable meanings ([Mikolov et al., 2013](https://aclanthology.org/N13-1090/))
    
-   The linear representation hypothesis holds in LLMs ([Park et al., 2024](https://arxiv.org/abs/2311.03658))
    
-   Linear probes can read off internal representations, e.g. world models in Othello-GPT ([Nanda et al., 2023](https://aclanthology.org/2023.blackboxnlp-1.2))
    
-   Steering vectors can causally influence model behavior ([Panickssery et al., 2024](https://arxiv.org/abs/2312.06681))
    
-   Ablation of single directions can remove capabilities like refusal ([Arditi et al., 2024](https://arxiv.org/abs/2406.11717))
    

MechInterp in Transformers – Superposition and SAEs:

-   Toy models show features are packed into fewer dimensions via superposition ([Elhage et al., 2022](https://arxiv.org/abs/2209.10652))
    
-   Mathematical background: compressed sensing motivates why sparse recovery works ([Candes et al., 2004](https://arxiv.org/abs/math/0409186); [Donoho, 2006](https://ieeexplore.ieee.org/document/1614066/))
    
-   Sparse autoencoders decompose superposed representations into interpretable features ([Bricken et al., 2023](https://transformer-circuits.pub/2023/monosemantic-features))
    
-   Evaluating SAE quality across architectures and metrics ([Karvonen et al., 2025](https://arxiv.org/abs/2503.09532))
    
-   Crosscoders: SAE variants that read/write across layers, enabling model diffing ([Lindsey et al., 2024](https://transformer-circuits.pub/2024/crosscoders/index.html))
    
-   Feature geometry, absorption, and splitting: failure modes of SAE feature learning ([Chanin et al., 2024](https://arxiv.org/abs/2409.14507))
    
-   Activation oracles: using LLMs to automatically label and explain SAE features ([Karvonen et al., 2025](https://arxiv.org/abs/2512.15674))
    

Circuit Discovery:

-   Success story: fully reverse-engineering a grokking model doing modular arithmetic ([Nanda et al., 2023](https://arxiv.org/abs/2301.05217))
    
-   Logit attribution: decomposing outputs by component, discovering induction heads ([Elhage et al., 2021](https://transformer-circuits.pub/2021/framework/index.html))
    
-   The logit lens: projecting intermediate residual stream states through the unembedding ([nostalgebraist, 2020](https://www.lesswrong.com/posts/AcKRB8wDpdaN6v6ru/interpreting-gpt-the-logit-lens))
    
-   Path patching: tracing causal pathways, discovering the IOI circuit ([Wang et al., 2022](https://arxiv.org/abs/2211.00593))
    
-   ACDC: automated circuit discovery via activation patching ([Conmy et al., 2023](https://arxiv.org/abs/2304.14997))
    
-   Attribution graphs: scalable circuit tracing revealing circuits like rhyming ([Lindsey et al., 2025](https://transformer-circuits.pub/2025/attribution-graphs/biology.html))
    
-   Causal scrubbing: a rigorous framework for validating circuit explanations ([Chan et al., 2022](https://www.lesswrong.com/posts/JvZhhzycHu2Yd57RN/causal-scrubbing-a-method-for-rigorously-testing))
    
-   Is circuit discovery NP-hard? Computational complexity of finding circuits ([Adolfi et al., 2025](https://arxiv.org/abs/2410.08025))
    

Outlook:

-   Real-world use: Claude Opus 4.5 system card used interp to investigate deceptive behavior ([Anthropic, 2025](https://www.anthropic.com/claude-opus-4-5-system-card))
    
-   Open problems in mechanistic interpretability ([Sharkey et al., 2025](https://arxiv.org/abs/2501.16496))
    

Critiques of MechInterp:

-   Pivoting from ambitious reverse-engineering to pragmatic, tool-oriented interp ([Nanda et al., 2025](https://www.alignmentforum.org/posts/StENzDcD3kpfGJssR/a-pragmatic-vision-for-interpretability))
    
-   Against almost every theory of impact of interpretability ([de Segerie, 2023](https://www.lesswrong.com/posts/LNA8mubrByG7SFacm/against-almost-every-theory-of-impact-of-interpretability-1))
